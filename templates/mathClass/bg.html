In this experiment, we are trying to test how teachers evaluate student ability- and what factors play a role in their decisions. To do this, we use data from the Early Childhood Longitudinal Study: Kindergarten Class of 2010-11 (hereafter ECLS).


The ECLS is a longitudinal dataset commissioned by the National Center for Education Statistics (NCES), that tracks a nationally representative sample of over 18000 children from kindergarten through fifth grade. he sample of students was chosen from the incoming kindergarten cohort of 2010-11, for both public and private schools. The study also incorporates information on the children's teachers, parents, and care-providers. Information on children is collected through a combination of direct cognitive assessments, detailed questionnaires for children, their teachers, their parents and care-providers, and from school-level administrative data. It also includes subjective and objective evaluations of each student, by different teachers through the child's progress from kindergarten to fifth grade.
